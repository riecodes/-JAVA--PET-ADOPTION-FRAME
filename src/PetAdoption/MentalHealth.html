What is Mental Health?

Learn about the definition and importance of mental health.

DEFINITION

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how people think, feel, and act, and helps determine how they handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.

IMPORTANCE

Good mental health is essential to overall well-being. It allows individuals to cope with the normal stresses of life, work productively, and contribute to their communities. Mental health problems are common but treatable. Many people experience mental health concerns from time to time, but a mental health concern becomes a mental illness when ongoing signs and symptoms cause frequent stress and affect a person's ability to function.

Resource sharing

Listen

Simply giving someone space to talk, and listening to how they're feeling, can be really helpful in itself. If they're finding it difficult, let them know that you're there when they are ready.

Offer reassurance

Seeking help can feel lonely, and sometimes scary. You can reassure someone by letting them know that they are not alone, and that you will be there to help.

Keep social contact

Part of the emotional support you offer could be to keep things as normal as possible. This could include involving your friend or family member in social events, or chatting about other parts of your lives.

Building a supportive community

Creating a Safe Space

In order to build a supportive community, it's important to create a safe space where individuals feel comfortable sharing their experiences and seeking support. Here are some strategies to consider:

  • Establish clear guidelines for respectful and inclusive communication.
  • Encourage active listening and empathy.
  • Provide resources and information about mental health.
  • Promote a non-judgmental and accepting environment.

Supportive Activities

Engaging in supportive activities can help foster a sense of community and well-being. Here are some ideas:

  • Organize group discussions of workshops on mental health topics.
  • Host virtual support groups where individuals can share their experiences and provide support to one another.
  • Encourage participation in community events or fundraisers related to mental health.
  • Share personal stories or testimonials to inspire and uplift others.

Building Connections

Building connections is essential for creating a supportive community. Here are some ways to foster connections:

  • Facilitate networking opportunities for individuals with similar interests or experiences.
  • Encourage collaboration and teamwork on projects or initiatives related to mental health.
  • Provide a platform for individuals to connect and communicate outside of formal meetings or events.
  • Organize social activities or outings to promote bonding and friendship.
